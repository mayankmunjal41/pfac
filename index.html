ABOUT US

Paws for a Cause is an animal welfare initiative by young people in Delhi who have chosen to combine their work as animal rescuers or adoptions coordinators under a common platform. Our aim is to spread the word about homeless and abandoned companion animals who need loving forever homes.

The process of adoption can be lengthy and difficult so our goal is to simplify things for those interested in it.

Adoption Process

Fill the Form

The people interested need to fill a form for the dog they are interested in. PfaC adoption form links are given in every Facebook post. Please make sure to fill out the adoption forms, clearly mentioning the name of the dog you're interested in adopting.

Contact Coordinator

After filling the forms, make sure to immediately send a message to the number provided in our posts. It is difficult for us to keep track of all our enquires since we have many cases at present, so keeping in touch with the adoption coordinator will help speed up the adoption procedure.

Schedule House Check

The next step requires you to schedule a housecheck with your adoption coordinator. For a housecheck, a volunteer from our organisation will visit your house, talk to you and your family members and look around your home.

Wait For a Decision

After the housecheck is conducted, our volunteer submits the housecheck report which is checked by our team and rescuers of the dog or members of the shelter where the dog currently is. A decision then is made whether the lead is fit for adoption.

Sign the Contract

If the team decides that the family is fit for adoption, the lead is required to pickup the dog from its current location and sign a foster contract ( 10 days minimum). After this, the adopters are required to fill our adoption contract. Our adoption contracts require the leads to fill out the form along with an address proof.

Send Happy Pictures

After the adoption procedure is completed, we ask the leads to send us pictures of the adopted kid enjoying at their home. Our hope is to inspire other people from these pictures to adopt from our organisation. Our happy adoption posts aim to tell the beautiful stories of our dogs finding the home they so rightfully deserve.

Adopt, Don't Shop
WHAT ?

Adoption is a process by which dogs who are incapable of surviving on the streets by themselves are provided with a home, love and care by some wonderful humans. Shopping is a process where dogs are bought from breeders.

What people don’t realise is that majority of breeders use unethical practices in the breeding process, the mother of the puppies is often raped excessively and lives an extremely traumatized life.
The mother usually dies during childbirth or is inhumanly discarded after she cannot bear any more puppies. Due to irresponsible inbreeding the puppies are extremely unhealthy, have a host of genetic diseases and usually develop multiple health problems later in life.

WHY YOU SHOULD ADOPT AND NOT SHOP ?

When you adopt a companion, you are saving a life

When you adopt you have the choice of choosing the age of your pet

When you adopt, you are consciously not supporting unethical and inhumane breeding practices When you adopt an animal, you are helping the shelter/ rescuer make space for other animals that need to be rescued

Adoption is an act of kindness and you are sure to receive a lot of love from all the animals you adopt

COMMON MYTHS!

Myth: Rescued/ shelter dogs are aggressive and will be difficult to handle

Truth: These dogs have usually been abandoned by irresponsible owners and all they require is some love, patience and training.

Myth: Older dogs/ cats will not bond with their new companions

Truth: Completely untrue, age is not a factor that determines whether animals with bond with people who are new to them. The amount of affection given, the time spent and the care will determine this.

Myth: Getting an animal from a breeder is safer because the breeder knows the animals bloodline and history

Truth: This is far from the truth. Most puppy mills which supply pet stores with puppies and breeders, often are extremely irresponsible and ignorant about breeding practices. They often fail to remove sick dogs from their breeding pools, as well as allow inbreeding to take place. This results in the puppies having many genetic diseases such as respiratory disorders, epilepsy, kidney and heart diseases.

Get to know Indies!
WHO ARE INDIES ?

Indian pariah dog, a dog breed native to India.

Pariahs or any cross-breeds of pariahs and other dogs get classified as Indies.

Indies have evolved through pure and simple 'survival of the fittest'. Without human interference, only the strongest and most intelligent of them have survived.

WHY INDIES ?

Indies are much more resilient, hardy, and resistant to most of the above problems. They are loving, friendly, intelligent and easily trainable.

Ability to handle the tropical Indian weather, and their agility in adapting to their surroundings.

They only shed their coats minimally and don't need too frequent groomer visits.

PROBLEMS WITH OTHER BREEDS

Purebred dogs have been genetically modified to breed certain characteristics that appeal to 'buyers'. Pugs get major breathing issues, eye problems and skin diseases. Dachshunds and Dalmatians suffer from back issues. Labradors are prone to obesity, arthritis, and hip dysplasia. Golden retrievers have the highest likelihood of developing cancer.

Due to unregulated and poor breeding practices, a lot of purebred puppies are born with genetic diseases and chronic infections.

Dogs not meant for Indian climates need extra care in summer/winter. High air-conditioning or heating bills. The furry breeds tend to shed quite a bit and get lots of dog hair, especially in shedding season, all over your furniture.

Why Sterilize ?
WHAT IS STERILIZATION?

The process of 'de-sexing' an animal is called sterilization.

For female dogs, it implies hysterectomy (removing the ovaries and uterus).

For male dogs, it means removal of the testicles.

WHEN TO STERILIZE?

The ideal time to sterilize a dog is just before their first 'heat' period - because that's when he/she is sexually mature and has grown to full maturity, but without any health issues arising from not sterilizing.

The exact timing can be checked with your vet, based on the dog's health.

WHY STERILIZE?

No chance of ovarian cysts, uterine infections, and cancer of the reproductive tract. Female dogs spayed before their first heat have a much lower chance of developing breast cancer.
There will be no heat cycles, so your female dog won't be howling in pain and making a 'mess' inside the house.

For male dogs, they have reduced chances of getting testicular cancer. Your male dog will not tend to wander far away, or spray urine to mark territory, and be less aggressive and territorial.

Dogs don't NEED sex. For them, it's a purely biological urge and a painful experience. The world does not need more homeless dogs. It's the responsible thing to do to break the chain, and not let your dog give birth to puppies that you will not be keeping.

COMMON MYTHS!

Myth:: Dogs gain weight after sterilization.
Fact: They gain weight due to lack of exercise and overfeeding. With some simple adjustments to their diets, they will do just fine.

Myth: It’s poses a risk to the overall and long-term health and well-being of the canine.
Fact:A safe and simple surgery, which is important for the health and happiness of your canine companion. Like any other surgery, there are minor risks, but then the risk of losing your beloved companion to cancer is much higher.

Can I foster a dog? What is the minimum number of days for fostering?

In order to foster a dog, you need to fill in our adoption/foster form. Please get in touch with the coordinator of the dog you wish to foster. A house check will be conducted following which you can foster that dog if the environment and experience of the foster is deemed suitable. A dog must be fostered for a minimum of 14 days, and a foster contract committing to this time period must be signed.

Can I meet a dog I’m interested in adopting?

Once the form has been filled and house check conducted, you are free to visit the dogs you are interested in adopting.

Do you also conduct rescues or ABC operations?

We function as an adoption agency, and therefore we do not conduct rescues as we do not run a shelter or centre. We also have no medical facilities or transportation van. However we will guide you on who to contact and what to do depending on the situation.

Is sterilization mandatory?

Sterilization is mandated as per our adoption contract. All shelters we work with also deem sterilization mandatory.

Do you charge an adoption fee?

While Paws for a Cause does NOT charge an adoption fee, some of the shelters we work with do charge a nominal amount. We do however function on donations, and would be grateful if anyone chooses to donate!

If I want to put my dog up for adoption, can I contact you?

You can contact us on Facebook or through our e-mail ID to discuss the situation with us.

Are rescuers allowed to be in touch with adopters?

This depends on the comfort of both the family that is adopting and the rescuer of the dog.

How long does it take for a dog to find a home?

We cannot guarantee adoptions within any specific time frame though we try our best to ensure no dog goes without a home for long. Purebred dogs, males, and young indie pups tend to get adopted faster due to breed, gender, and age biases.
