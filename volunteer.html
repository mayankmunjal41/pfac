VOLUNTEER INFORMATION

Who can volunteer?

Anyone willing to dedicate time and effort in helping us change the lives of the dogs who we can help. We believe the best volunteer is one who is looking to make a difference.

How do volunteers work?

We do not run a shelter so everyday physical presence is not required. Most of our work is done virtually so volunteers need to be tech savvy and regularly responsive online.

What kind of volunteer can you be?

One can volunteer for more than one more depending on one’s ability to contribute.

  • • Adoption Coordinator
  • • Every coordinator is assigned a maximum of 3 cases whereby they are responsible for finding homes for the dogs.
  • • The coordinator acts as the contact person between the potential adopter family and the rescuer/shelter. Regular communication is important.
  • • Coordinators need to be very active in following up on leads, getting the dog photographed for online adoption appeals, setting up house checks and updating the information spreadsheets.
  • • They need to be prepared to manage all parties involved in a case and act quickly in cases of emergency.
  • • House Check Volunteer
  • • The volunteer will be responsible for conducting house checks and sending us a report to help us determine whether the family is suitable for the dog they wish to adopt.
  • • The volunteer plays an important role in explaining the specific needs of the dog as well as in answering any questions which the family may have.
  • • The volunteer will be asked to conduct house checks primarily within the area they are comfortable with.
  • • Social Media Volunteer
  • • This involves managing our Facebook page, Instagram account and website.
  • • The volunteer will be responsible for maintaining the quality of the content which goes up on our online platforms.
  • • The volunteer will also have to maintain the schedule of our online posts and coordinate with all team members.
  • • Photographer
  • • The photographer will be responsible to taking photographs of dogs which need to be posted for adoption as well as events organized by PfaC.
  • • Graphic Designer
  • • The graphic designer will have to help create designs for our social media platforms as well as our merchandise, sold to raise funds for our dogs.

All our volunteer work pro bono, we work on donations and are thus, unable to pay our volunteers. Before volunteering, we ask people to remember that this is actual work, not a past time. We function as an adoption agency and help change lives, so we are only looking for people who are dedicated and understand that some time every day is needed to work on their cases. If you feel you have enough desire to help animals and can put in time, please fill out the form and inform us when it has been done.

Recruitment form link: https://goo.gl/forms/mfBjDodMkUVk1D632

We add people every few months so hearing back may take some time. We add people based on their form responses so please give them proper thought. If you still do not hear from us please message us on our social media platforms.

Thank you!

Adoption Process

Fill the Form

The people interested need to fill a form for the dog they are interested in. PfaC adoption form links are given in every Facebook post. Please make sure to fill out the adoption forms, clearly mentioning the name of the dog you're interested in adopting.

Contact Coordinator

After filling the forms, make sure to immediately send a message to the number provided in our posts. It is difficult for us to keep track of all our enquires since we have many cases at present, so keeping in touch with the adoption coordinator will help speed up the adoption procedure.

Schedule House Check

The next step requires you to schedule a housecheck with your adoption coordinator. For a housecheck, a volunteer from our organisation will visit your house, talk to you and your family members and look around your home.

Wait For a Decision

After the housecheck is conducted, our volunteer submits the housecheck report which is checked by our team and rescuers of the dog or members of the shelter where the dog currently is. A decision then is made whether the lead is fit for adoption.

Sign the Contract

If the team decides that the family is fit for adoption, the lead is required to pickup the dog from its current location and sign a foster contract ( 10 days minimum). After this, the adopters are required to fill our adoption contract. Our adoption contracts require the leads to fill out the form along with an address proof.

Send Happy Pictures

After the adoption procedure is completed, we ask the leads to send us pictures of the adopted kid enjoying at their home. Our hope is to inspire other people from these pictures to adopt from our organisation. Our happy adoption posts aim to tell the beautiful stories of our dogs finding the home they so rightfully deserve.
